RESERVE BANK OF INDIA
PMLA COMPLIANCE DIRECTIVE - 2024

SECTION 3.1: LARGE CASH INGESTION Thresholds
Any transaction exceeding INR 10,00,000 (roughly 120,500 USD) or cash ingestion above INR 8,30,000 from a flagged high-risk origin requires immediate managerial approval and manual audit. Suspicious activity flags (PMLA-S1) in the ledger must also trigger an AML alert.

SECTION 3.2: SMURFING AND STRUCTURING LIMITS
Continuous transfers between INR 8,000 and INR 10,000 from a single account within a single dataset shall be considered structuring behavior (smurfing). These must be flagged for manual review to detect evasion of the reporting threshold.

SECTION 3.3: VELOCITY AND RAPID SUCCESSION
More than 10 transactions initiated from the same account holder within a 24-hour cycle constitute a velocity breach. Such high-frequency activity indicates automated scraping or layering and must be halted.

SECTION 4.1: CROSS-BORDER REMITTANCE
All outward remittances in non-USD currencies (Euro, GBP, etc.) exceeding a value of 600 USD must undergo enhanced KYC verification for local compliance within 24 hours of execution.

SECTION 4.2: CASH REPORTING DUTY
Any cash-based transaction above the INR 2,00,000 (roughly 2410 USD) individual limit must be reported to the Principal Officer on the same business day to ensure PMLA reporting standards.

SECTION 5.1: ASSET CONCENTRATION AND MULE DETECTION
Destination accounts receiving funds from more than 10 unique source accounts (mule behavior) must be flagged for enhanced due diligence. Layering techniques involving disparate sources into a single merchant are prohibited.

SECTION 5.2: ROUND NUMBER PROFILING
Exactly rounded transactions (e.g., INR 10,000, INR 50,000) that occur at high volumes suggest structured laundering profiles and require an immediate AML pattern review.
